## Comments {#_comments}

There is an introductory remark written by a clerk before the report's
text, in which the clerk says that copies of:

- this report

- the chamber rescript (decision)

- the related purchase deeds

were taken **to create the Stätteacten** (settlement files) for:

- No. 13 Nordholz

- No. 14 Nordholz

under the archival series:

> Bauergüter & Colonien im Amte Bückeburg

So this note is basically saying:

> These documents were copied and placed in the individual property
> files for those two settlements.

``` {role="bordered"}
No. 257 — 11.)

Bückeburg, 15 June 1818
M. Kaas

See Kammer file no. 62 from 1817

Recorded in the Kammer’s document register as entry no. 237; see also
entries 33 and 136

To the Princely Chamber
Report of the Offices of Bückeburg and Arensburg
concerning
land assignment

Memorandum. From this report, from the following Chamber rescript, and from the
relevant purchase deeds, copies have been taken for the formation of files for the
settlement holdings (Nos. 13 and 14 in Nordholz) under the series Bauergüter &
Colonien in the Office of Bückeburg.

Concerning the land assignments ordered under the previously cited Chamber numbers
for Krückeberg No. 10 in Berenbusch and for the two new settlers Anton Otto Voigt
and Gottlieb Brüggemann, Nos. 13 and 14 in Nordholz, and carried out by us on
9 March of this year in the presence of the district forestry officer, we herewith
submit the survey drawing; and since for the new settler Brüggemann, under No. C.
101, the price per rod to be paid for one-half Morgen has been fixed at 6 Groschen
5 Pfennig per rod, and the remaining land at 12 Groschen per rod, payment is to be
made accordingly.

P. N.
The dues are to be paid after the expiration of three free years, therefore no
later than Michaelmas 1822.
M. Kaas

Bückeburg, 30 May 1818
Habicht Neufsel
```
