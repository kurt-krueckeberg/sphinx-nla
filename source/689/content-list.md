# Contents List

The number in the first column of the contents list designates a cluster of related documents in which one aspect
of the overall decision making process is under consideration. The 2nd column describes each document, the third
column is a note on the subject of the doucment(s); lastly comes the place, year, month and day

```{figure} images/01-r.png
```

## Transliteration

The first column represents a **procedural unit /docket item (Actenpunkt)**.
Each Actenpunkt corresponds to **one administrative step in the handling of
the matter.**, and that step may generate one document or several (petition
→ report → resolution → rescript, etc.).

**Note:** The column “Z.” denotes the sequential registry number within the
stated month, not the day of the month.

::::{sidebar} Carifications

- **#3**--The **"Acten Designation (right)"** column has "gegen" (in "gegen Verpfändung seiner
  sämmtlichen Ländereien"). **"gegen"** typically means "agasint"; here it menas "on
condition of". The Rentcammer was willing to consider a loan but only against the pledge
of his entire holding. The Chamber’s willingness to approve a loan was conditional upon
his pledging all of his landholdings as collateral.
- **#7**--**aus** in **Cpt. K. Res. aus Amt Bückeburg** is incorrect. The actual document has
**an** not **aus**: **Concept an das Amt Bückeburg**, bears no **pr. in Cam.**
(incoming-presented) mark, and carries the Kammer closing **Fürstl: p.**. **aus** is retained
in the transliteration for accuracy, but rendered as **to the Amt Bückeburg** in the English
translation, noting the likely clerical slip.
- **#10**--is **not a memo**; it’s the **Obligation (the sealed promissory note itself)**.
Originals of **promissory notes** were often **kept in a separate “obligations” series** and,
after repayment, **returned to the debtor**—so they commonly **don’t carry** the file’s boxed
corner number and may not be physically in the case file anymore.  *That’s why #10 has no
boxed number in the case file.*

::::
