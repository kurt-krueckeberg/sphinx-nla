# Untitled

The heading at the top of Document #4 clearly reads:

> **“An Vormundschaftliche Kammer Bericht der Aemter Bückeburg und Arensburg.”**
>
> --  heading

That confirms that even in 1800 the **Ausweisung** (land-assignment) paperwork
still went first through the **Vormundschaftliche Kammer**.

Here’s what’s happening in plain English:

- The **Bückeburg Amt** is sending the finished **survey drawing and area
calculation** for the land added to Jobst Henrich Krückeberg’s garden and
yard.
- It is being sent **to the Vormundschaftliche Kammer**, because that
chamber had to **approve the legal side** of the assignment — who may hold
the land, that no neighbor’s rights are violated, and that the proper
procedure was followed.
- After approval, the **Rentkammer** would later handle the **financial
side** (recording rent, fees, and the purchase contract).

So, even though this document deals with measured land (**Forstsachen**), it
is addressed to the **Vormundschaftliche Kammer** because that chamber
issued the **legal authorization** before the fiscal paperwork proceeded.
