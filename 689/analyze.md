Since the Acten Designation items 1–7 all fall in **1841**, use this tight checklist of what to extract from each document

## What to note on each document (quick audit list)

1. **Document type:**
  **Vorstellung / Gsuch / Bericht / Official Order (Rescr./“Official Order of the Chamber”**
and to/from.
2. **Dates (all of them)**:
  Body date, **“pr. in Cam”** (presented in Chamber), countersign dates, and any **expedition date** on slips.
3. **Numbers/marks:**
  - **Nr pr.** (protocol number) and where it sits on the leaf.
  - **N.C. *#*#** at the top (if present): note the number and date → check if it’s a **running Chamber docket** (does it rise with later dates?).
  - Any **former/older identifiers** on covers (for later concordance).
4. **Enclosure signals:**
  - **Anb.** on register line or leaf.
  - Phrases like **“anbei zurückerfolgende Bittschrift”**, **“Beilage/Anlage/Abschrift”**, **“Beil. A/B/…”**.
  - Name the enclosed item (e.g., **Situations-Riß**, judgment copy).
5. **Right-column “Topic note” alignment:**
  Confirm the right column’s short note (loan amount, pledge condition, sale request) matches the body content.
6. **Decision language:**
  Look for formulas: **“findet keine Statt”** (does not take place/denied), **“gestattet werden solle”** (shall be permitted),
**“zu bescheiden”** (to decide), **“Höchstes Rescr.”** (Highest Official Order). Capture the **exact wording**.
7. **Security/collateral details:**
  Any mention of **“gegen Verpfändung … sämtlicher Ländereien”**, acreage, insurance value, existing debts,
valuation—-these explain conditions for the loan.
8. **Transmission chain**
  Endorsements/cover slips: **“Copt/Cpt.”**, routing (to Amt, to officer Creuzinger), “Expedirt” notes, initials.
9. **Scribal notes****Conc. [name]** (Concipist/drafter), **m[anu] p[ropria]**; signatures of officials (Hauck, Dr. Neuhsel, Höcker, etc.).
10. **Marginalia & foliation:** Later pencil folios vs. ink notes; keep separate from 1841 hands.
