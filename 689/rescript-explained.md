:::{admonition} Rescript Explained

A Rescript is abbreviated in the Acten Desingation, as " “Rescr.” or “KRes.” (Kammer-Rescript). in the Acten
Designation. It   is a **written official reply or order** issued by a higher authority in response to a petition,
report, or request.

In a legal context: it’s an **authoritative decision communicated in writing**.  In a financial-administrative
context, which is the case here, it’s a **formal directive, decree, or ruling.**

The best translation is "Official Order of the Chamber” or “Decree of the Chamber”.

**Etymology**:

In classical and medieval Latin, **rescriptum**  literally means “something written back” (re-scribere). In
Roman and later European law it **meant a written reply by the ruler** to a petition. German chancery practice
inherited the term directly from Latin, and it shows up in 18th–19th century files as Rescriptum or abbreviated
Rescr.  Rescript is simply the shortened form of the Latin Rescriptum, borrowed into German (Reskript) and then
into English.

:::
